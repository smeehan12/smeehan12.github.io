Who am I?

I am an enthusiastic and creative individual with 15 years of experience in academic and scientific research, technical projects, community service, and education at all levels. I have worked at institutions around the globe with people coming from a diverse array of backgrounds. In all aspects of my life, I strive to improve the welfare of every individual and our global community while empowering others to do likewise. I think I do this best as a "technical handyman", "people person", "professional chit-chatter"

Technical Handyman

Big data and problem solving

I have a PhD in particle physics which I pursued out of an intrinsic curiosity to understand the fundamental laws of nature. During the course of 12 years of study and post-doctoral research, I developed the ability to manipulate and understand big data sets, to construct and operate high speed electronics, and draw meaningful insights into the natural world. I enjoy asking questions, defining ill-posed problems, and finding solutions. I relish the excitement of discovery - both when I learn a new skill or when I can help bring to light a new principle of the universe.

People Person

Dynamic leader and mentor

In tandem, I have cultivated a set of “soft skills” resulting from the environment in which my work took place. Particle physics is a domain which involves large international collaborations, is both cooperative and competitive, and inherently relies on individuals learning (and teaching) while pursuing their researching. As such, I have developed strong sense of time management and group leadership skills. I know how to prioritize my work to pursue multiple projects in parallel and guide a team to success. I know how to communicate and how to facilitate communication both within a team and I know the value of mentoring colleagues and cultivating talent.

Professional Chit-chatter

Critical thinking and dialogue to learn and grow

I enjoy talking and I enjoy learning and I value the freedom of expression. I appreciate family, friends, and colleagues who are willing to share what they know and willing to challenge that which I think I know. I have come to embrace the paradigm of institutional disconfirmation (i.e. healthy skepticism) and feel that this forms a constructive cornerstone for every discussion, professional or otherwise. My scientific training has provided me with a strong intuition for separating fact from opinion, rooting arguments in fact and reason, and acknowledging when my own belief should be updated.

What do I do?

I am, by training, an academic. To me, this means both pursuing and creating knowledge while cultivating it in others. My curiosity led me to pursue physics and it persists to boggle my mind that we can begin to comprehend the natural world (... let alone anything). But all of this is pointless without the welfare of "the other". As such, I have intrinsically been drawn to activities and initiatives involving other people. My professional pursuits can broadly be thought of as following three intermingled prongs.

Science

Some details about my science experience

Big Data @ ATLAS

I search for new fundamental particles and forces (e.g. dark matter) not currently described in the paradigm of particle physics. This work leverages complex statistical models to describe enormous datasets.

  • Details

Plumbing @ ATLAS

I automate data analysis pipeline workflows for physics searches and detector calibration for to improve efficiency turnaround time of our collaborations data analysis.

  • Details

ML with Jets @ ATLAS

I study, develop, and implement techniques to identify the mother particle (e.g. gluon, top quark, Higgs boson) of multi-particle showers called “jets”. These methods often involve machine learning to take full advantage of the information within our data.

  • Details

People @ ATLAS

I have served multiple times as leader of ATLAS research groups focusing on (i) dark matter searches, (ii) hadronic jet tagging team, and (iii) Higgs boson tagging. These are diverse groups of up to 100 students and scientists.

  • Details

Data Readout @ FASER

I am helping to construct the FASER experiment to search for ultra-long-lived particles at the LHC. I design and implement the data acquisition system that will ultimately collect and store all of the data from this experiment.

  • Details

Ultra-fast Detectors

In graduate school, I contributed to the design of first generation ASICs for front-end readout of large-area photo detectors with picosecond timing resolution to measure things occurring one millionth of a millionth of a second apart.

  • Details

Sleep Science of Worms

Early in graduate school, I studied the sleep-like state in simple organisms (e.g C. elegans nematode) by constructing a pulsed laser system to perform non-invasive dissection of the nervous system to control its behavior.

  • Details

Flexible Detectors

My first introduction to experimental research was an immersion in understanding the behavior of micromesh-gaseous detectors during an internship in France. Ten years later, and my current experiment (ATLAS) is now installing this type of detector for use.

  • Details

String Theory

My very first introduction to research. I swung for the fences and tried to understand the underpinnings of the universe as viewed by string theory. I have become considerably more empirical since those days, and only more curious if this is “truth”.

  • Details
